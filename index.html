Hey Boaters,

Nevada boating season has arrived! The Nevada Department of Wildlife wants you to have a safe and fun boating experience no matter which lake or waterway you choose. We also ask that you join us this season to stop aquatic hitchhikers from spreading to other bodies of water. Remember to do your part: clean, drain, dry and get your AIS decal. After all, it is the law. Together, we can keep our lakes healthy and clean for years to come.

IT'S THE LAW

Nevada Revised Statute 488.045 requires the owner, operator or person in control of any vessel must ensure that the drain plugs, drain valves, and any other devices used to control the draining of water remain open while transporting the vessel on public roads in the State of Nevada

DO YOUR PART

Stop Aquatic Hitchhikers like quagga mussels from spreading to othe water by visiting our AIS removal stations and always remember to
Clean, Drain and Dry... Every Time and Everywhere.

CLEAN

Remove all mud, plants and animals from every part of your boat.

DRAIN

Remove all water from your boat, including its live - wells before you leave the recreation area.

DRY

Allow boat to completely dry before you launch in another waterway.
